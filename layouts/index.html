The Problem We Solve

Women are underrepresented on stages, in media, and in leadership — not because of ability, but because of broken discovery and visibility infrastructure.

For Women

  • Less visible than male peers despite equal or greater expertise
  • Invited less frequently to speak publicly
  • Underrepresented on panels and at conferences
  • Lack structured guidance for building visibility
  • Rely heavily on personal networks for opportunities

For Organizations

  • Want diverse speakers but struggle to find them quickly
  • Repeatedly invite the same known profiles
  • Lack structured ways to discover new voices
  • Need support building balanced panels
  • Cannot easily filter experts by industry, language, or expertise

Our Story

The name SISY references the myth of Sisyphus — never-ending work — but reframes it as relentless, purposeful progress. SISY is built on the belief that expertise should be discoverable, visibility should be structured, and the infrastructure for female representation should be as strong as it is for any other group.

The Background

Women are interrupted more often, spoken over in meetings, and invited less frequently to speak — not because of ability, but because of systemic visibility gaps. Highly qualified female voices exist in every industry, but the infrastructure to discover and surface them is broken. SISY was built to fix that.

The Idea

What if expertise was structured, searchable, and public by default? What if organizations had real tools to build genuinely balanced panels? What if the path from "qualified expert" to "recognized voice" was a supported journey, not an accident? That's what SISY builds.
